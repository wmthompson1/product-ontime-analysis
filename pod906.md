The SQLMesh backfill mechanism is the "Systemic Solder" that makes your **Masking Matrix** enforceable across the entire timeline of your data. Since we are currently addressing a logic error where the wrong fields were masked, the **Backfill** is the tool we use to "Elevate" the restored raw data into a consistent, hashed state that respects your new DBA requirements.

### 🛠️ Why Backfills Matter for the Masking Matrix
In a **Theory of Constraints (ToC)** framework, the backfill prevents "Data Inertia" by ensuring that old records aren't left with raw PII or inconsistent hashes after you update the matrix:

* **Masking Matrix Updates**: When you add `LAST_NAME` or `FIRST_NAME` to your `pii_targets.csv`, the backfill identifies every existing snapshot of those 16 staging models and re-runs the `mask_pii.py` logic to bring them into compliance.
* **Dimensional Consistency**: Because `stg_vendors` is Level 0 and `stg_parts` is Level 1, a backfill ensures that if a Vendor ID changes its hash due to a new salt, the Parts table is automatically re-processed to maintain the join.
* **Schema Evolution**: As you transition from Faux data to Hashed data, the backfill acts as the "Parity Gate," replacing synthetic values with the deterministic hashes defined in your SQLite hierarchy.
