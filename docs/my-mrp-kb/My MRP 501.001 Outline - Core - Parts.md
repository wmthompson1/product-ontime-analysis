**Detail and Standards (not raw material)**

**In manufacturing, a detail part is a custom-engineered component manufactured specifically for a unique assembly, often made to order via custom drawings. Conversely, a standard part (or "off-the-shelf" item) is a pre-manufactured, interchangeable component (like bolts, bearings, or resistors) designed for general use, high-volume consistency, and ease of sourcing. **Key Differences:**

- **Detail Part (Custom/Non-Standard):**
  - **Definition: Fabricated specifically to engineering specifications (drawings) for a particular assembly.**
  - **Production: Generally made-to-order, often with tighter tolerances or specialized materials.**
  - **Control: The manufacturer or designer dictates the material, process, and dimensions.**
  - **Context: Requires a detailed, unique part number, but is not a finished, salable end item.**
- **Standard Part:**
  - **Definition: A commonly available item that adheres to established industry specifications (ISO, DIN, ANSI, etc.).**
  - **Production: High-volume, repeatable, and consistent.**
  - **Control: Manufactured by a third party with standardized parameters.**
  - **Context: Used across multiple products to reduce inventory costs and simplify assembly.
