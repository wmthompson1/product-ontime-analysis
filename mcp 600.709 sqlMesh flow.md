# Understanding the Masking DAG Lifecycle

The synchronization of masking matrix changes within a data pipeline is not merely a configuration update but a traversal through a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG). This narrative explains how changes propagate across the trust boundary from pre-stage to receiving, ensuring that PII constraints are maintained at every node.

**Example Scenario: The Vendor-Part Relationship**  
At the root of the DAG is the **dbo.Vendor** table. Per corpus policy, sensitive fields such as *Vendor ID* are masked in the pre-stage environment before any downstream models can ingest them.

**Chronology of a Masking Update**

1. **08:00 AM – Matrix Update:** A change is committed to the masking matrix for model **1.2 (Part)**. This update defines new dimensionality or hashing rules for the *pref\_vendor* field.  
2. **09:00 AM – Boundary Inspection:** The **Part** table passes inspection at the pre-stage border. SQLMesh analyzes the upstream dependency (Vendor) to ensure the data is cleared for movement.  
3. **09:15 AM – Certification & Application:** The certification process at receiving mandates that the masking be applied per the version 1.2 rules. SQLMesh executes a plan to rebuild the masked dimensionality overlay.
